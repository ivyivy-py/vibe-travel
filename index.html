Cash Allowance

Most countries have regulations on the amount of cash you can carry across their borders without declaring it. This amount is typically around $10,000 USD, but it can vary. Check the customs website of your destination country for specific regulations.

Travel Insurance

It is highly recommended to have travel insurance. Popular options include World Nomads, IMG, and Allianz.

Dress Code

Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. In general, casual and comfortable clothing is acceptable for most tourist areas. For upscale restaurants or events, a smart casual dress code is often expected.

Alcohol Consumption

The laws and social norms regarding alcohol consumption vary widely. In some countries, alcohol is readily available, while in others, it is restricted or prohibited. Be aware of the local laws to avoid any legal issues.

Transportation Options

Most destinations offer a variety of transportation options, including public transit (buses, trains, subways), taxis, and ride-sharing services. For more flexibility, you might consider renting a car.

Safety

Stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect your belongings. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas. It's also a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and trip cancellations.

Places to Go

Explore a mix of popular tourist attractions and off-the-beaten-path gems. Research in advance to create an itinerary that matches your interests, whether it's historical sites, natural wonders, or culinary experiences.
